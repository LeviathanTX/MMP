The problem

Millions of forgotten wells are still leaking.

Across the United States, end-of-life and abandoned wells vent methane into the atmosphere long after they stop producing. Public funding to seal them falls far short of the need — which is exactly why the carbon market has a role to play.

How it works

From liability to verified asset.

Every step is measured, documented, and audit-ready — so the credits we generate hold up to the scrutiny serious buyers demand.

Baseline

We measure each well's methane emissions before any work begins, establishing a verified baseline.

Plug

Qualified crews permanently seal the wellbore to accepted plug-and-abandonment standards.

Verify

Post-plugging monitoring confirms emissions have stopped, reviewed by an independent body.

Issue

Verified reductions become insured carbon credits, tracked transparently end to end.

Credit quality

Built on the three things buyers actually check.

Voluntary carbon markets have grown wary of low-quality offsets. Methane abatement through well plugging meets the bar on every count.

Transparency

Measured, not modeled

Emissions are quantified with digital monitoring and documented under recognized methodologies — you can see how every credit was derived.

Permanence

Sealed for good

A plugged well doesn't leak again. Post-plugging verification confirms the methane stays in the ground permanently.

Additionality

Wouldn't happen otherwise

These wells sit outside committed public funding. Without the carbon market, they stay open and keep emitting.

About

Built by someone who grew up around these wells.

Brennan HensleyFOUNDER & CEO

Brennan was born and raised in Midland, in the heart of the Texas oil patch. The wells we plug aren't an abstraction to him — they're the landscape he grew up in.

After more than two decades building revenue, partnerships, and teams across energy, environmental services, finance, and technology — with leadership roles at CoStar Group, Newtek Business Services, and Transect — he founded Methane Mitigation Partners to take on a problem he understands from both sides: the operator who carries the liability, and the buyer who needs credits that actually hold up.

That combination — oil-patch roots and a career spent closing deals between parties who don't naturally speak the same language — is the whole point. Plugging a well only happens when the operator, the measurement, the verifier, and the buyer all line up. Brennan's job is making that line up.

He brings a practical, relationship-driven approach to every project, and works directly with operators and credit buyers to build outcomes that work for both.
